
In order to make probability statements about ω given y, we must begin with a model providing a joint probability distribution for ω and y. The joint probability mass or density function can be written as a product of two densities that are oftenreferredtoastheprior distribution p(ω)andthesampling distribution (or data distribution) p(y|ω), respectively: p(ω,y)=p(ω)p(y|ω)
